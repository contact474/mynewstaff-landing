Common Questions

FAQ

Does Brooke actually have a visible face on the call? +
Yes. Brooke appears as a video participant with a moving, lip-synced avatar face. She looks like a real person on camera. Prospects don't know they're talking to AI unless you tell them.
What platforms does Brooke work on? +
Zoom, Google Meet, and Microsoft Teams. Any platform that accepts a meeting join link. We're adding Webex and custom SIP in Q2 2026.
How does Brooke handle objections? +
Brooke is trained on the NEPQ (Neuro-Emotional Persuasion Questioning) framework by Jeremy Miner. She doesn't push or pitch. She asks calibrated questions that let the prospect sell themselves. When objections come up, she acknowledges, probes deeper, and reframes — exactly how a top 1% closer would.
Can I customize Brooke's persona? +
On Pro and Enterprise plans, you can build custom personas. Define her name, voice, personality, product knowledge, qualification criteria, and follow-up behavior. She becomes YOUR employee — with your company's knowledge and your sales playbook.
What happens after the call? +
You get a full recording, transcript, and AI-generated summary with key insights, qualification score, and recommended next steps. On Pro+, this syncs directly to your CRM. Brooke can also send a personalized follow-up email within 60 seconds of hanging up.
Is this legal / compliant? +
Brooke operates within all standard video call recording consent laws. She identifies as an AI assistant at the start of each call (configurable). You're responsible for compliance with your local recording consent regulations, just like with any call recording tool.
How much does each call cost? +
Underlying cost is $0.04/minute. On Starter ($500/mo for 50 calls), that averages about $10/call for a 15-minute conversation. Compare that to a $4,500/mo SDR who can make maybe 8 quality calls a day. The math speaks for itself.
Can Brooke handle multiple calls at once? +
Yes. On Enterprise, Brooke can be on 10+ calls simultaneously. Each instance is fully independent with its own context. Your bottleneck stops being headcount.
